# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to building a professional, inclusive, and respectful open-source community around CsvForge.

Participants are expected to contribute in ways that foster collaboration, trust, and technical excellence.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful, constructive, and considerate in all interactions.
- Assume good intent and discuss ideas, not individuals.
- Provide actionable technical feedback.
- Accept accountability and correct mistakes promptly.
- Prioritize community health alongside project outcomes.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, threats, or discriminatory language.
- Personal attacks, insults, or repeated hostile conduct.
- Publishing private information without explicit permission.
- Deliberate disruption of discussions, reviews, or community channels.
- Any conduct that would be considered unprofessional in a workplace setting.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all CsvForge project spaces, including:

- GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, and reviews
- Official chat/community channels
- Conference talks, social posts, and other public representations of the project

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, report it to:

- **maintainers@csvforge.dev**

Please include relevant context (links, screenshots, timestamps) where possible.

## Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for enforcing this Code of Conduct fairly and consistently.

Possible enforcement actions include:

1. Private warning
2. Temporary restrictions from project participation
3. Permanent ban from project spaces

Enforcement decisions will prioritize community safety and may be made without public disclosure of private details.
